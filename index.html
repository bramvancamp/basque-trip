The Shape of It

Land in Bilbao, loop south to the vines, finish in San Sebastián.

The route is built around short hops — never more than 75 minutes behind the wheel — so the car is a tool, not the trip. You fly into Bilbao, spend two nights eating your way through the Casco Viejo, drop south into the Rioja Alavesa for a night among medieval walls and avant-garde wineries, then point northeast to San Sebastián, the gastronomic capital of Europe.

Two beginner-friendly surf sessions bookend the food: Sopelana on the way in, Zarautz on the way out. Both fly home from Bilbao, an easy run from the coast.

The Itinerary
01
Wed 15 Jul
Bilbao

Arrive, decompress, eat 🍽️

You both land mid-afternoon — pick up the car together, drive the 15 minutes into the Casco Viejo, and check in. With a 16:00–16:30 arrival there's just enough time to drop bags, walk the riverfront and find a cold txakoli before the evening's main event: a pintxos crawl through Plaza Nueva. The ritual is one drink, one or two bites, then move on. Start around 19:30 and eat until you can't.

Where to stay

Or any Casco Viejo boutique to be steps from the bars ~€80–120/night

02
Thu 16 Jul
Bilbao

Clifftop hike, first waves 🌊, big dinner

HikeSurf · beginner

Start the morning with the Uribe Kosta coastal path — a 10 km clifftop trail running from Plentzia to Sopelana along dramatic flysch sea-cliffs with views out over the Bay of Biscay. Allow 2.5–3 hours at an easy pace; the trail ends right at the beach. Then stay put: paddle straight into a surf lesson at Sopelana on the gentle summer beach-break. ≈ €45–55 for a 2h session.

Evening, back in Bilbao: skip the bar crawl and sit down for a proper txuletón — the enormous bone-in Basque ribeye, charcoal-grilled, served for two. Asador Indusi or Etxanobe both deliver. ≈ €40–60 each with wine.

03
Fri 17 Jul
Rioja Alavesa

South to the vines 🍷 & the walled town

WineView

A 45-minute drive drops you into a different world: vineyards to the horizon, the Sierra de Cantabria behind, medieval villages on the hilltops, almost no tourists. Morning at Bodega Ysios, Calatrava's wave-roofed winery against the mountains. Then Marqués de Riscal in Elciego — Frank Gehry's titanium showpiece, by the same hand as the Guggenheim. The jump from ancient village to avant-garde architecture is jarring in the best way.

Afternoon: walk Laguardia — the walls, the calados (wine cellars carved beneath the streets), the view over the vines. Long, slow lunch with a bottle of Tempranillo. No rush; you're sleeping here.

Where to stay

04
Sat 18 Jul
San Sebastián

Zurriola 🏄 surf, La Concha & the pintxos capital

Surf · beginnerView

A 1h15 drive northeast to San Sebastián. Check into Gros, dump bags, and head straight to Zurriola beach — the surf beach that's practically your front door — for a mid-morning lesson on the Atlantic beach-break. ≈ €45–55 for a 2h session. After lunch, walk the La Concha promenade and hike up Monte Urgull (free, 30 min) for the panorama over the bay. Then the reason you came: the Parte Vieja crawl, where pintxos are more refined, fresher, and higher-average than anywhere else on earth.

Where to stay
Gros district — calmer than the old town, by Zurriola surf beach, walkable to everything ~€100–150/night

Sun 20 Jul
Zarautz → BIO

One more wave 🌊, then home

Surf · beginner

Drive 25 minutes west to Zarautz, the quintessential Spanish surf town — a 2.5km beach lined with surf schools. A final morning session on forgiving summer waves, lunch on the promenade, then the easy 1h10 run back to Bilbao airport to drop the car and fly home.

Zarautz — the Basque coast's longest beach, a surfer's town
🌊 The Waves — Beginner Only

Summer is the right time to learn here.

Day 2 · 20 min from Bilbao

Sopelana

A wide sandy beach at Arrietara with gentle, rolling waves and multiple peaks made for learning. Surf schools right on the sand handle boards and wetsuits. ≈ €45–55 for a 2h lesson.

Day 4 · Gros district, San Sebastián

Zurriola

The surf beach of San Sebastián, right in the Gros neighbourhood where you're staying. More exposed than Sopelana — a step up in energy. Multiple schools on the sand. ≈ €45–55 for a 2h lesson.

Day 5 · 25 min from Donostia

Zarautz

The longest beach on the Basque coast — 2.5km, a reliable beach break working best at mid-to-high tide, and a promenade full of surf schools. Lessons ≈ €45–55.

July swell is small and inconsistent — which is exactly what makes it ideal for first-timers. Warm water, forgiving waves, no crowds in the lineup. Book lessons a few days ahead; schools fill up in summer.

Before You Go

Six things worth knowing

📅

Book the Guggenheim & wineries ahead

July is busy and timed slots fill up. Reserve Ysios and Marqués de Riscal tours online before you fly.

⏰

La Cuchara takes no reservations

Arrive at 19:30 when it opens, or be prepared to wait shoulder-to-shoulder.

🕢

Eat pintxos at 7:30–9pm

The liveliest session. Dodge the 8:30–9:30 tourist peak if you want elbow room at the bar.

🏄

Book surf a few days out

Schools fill in July. Lock Sopelana and Zarautz in advance rather than walking up.

🥂

Pour the txakoli high

The local fizzy white is poured from height to aerate it. Order it everywhere; it's made for pintxos.

🚗

One car, from the start

Same arrival airport — coordinate flight times and split the rental from the moment you both land.

Festival de Jazz de Vitoria-Gasteiz

Vitoria-Gasteiz · 60 km from Bilbao · 45 min drive

One of Europe's great jazz festivals, running for 50+ years. Free daytime concerts pack the Plaza de España; paid evening headline sets bring world-class names. Perfectly combinable with the Rioja Alavesa day — it's a 45-minute drive from Laguardia.

Wine & Culture Summer weekends · Jul–Aug

Laguardia Summer Evenings

Laguardia · your Day 3 base

The medieval walled town comes alive on summer Friday and Saturday nights — live folk and jazz in the main square, local wine poured from the barrel, stalls selling Rioja products. You're sleeping inside the walls, so you just walk downstairs.

🍷 ✦ Fri 18 Jul — you're there
Surf & Beach Throughout July

Zarautz Summer Beach Events

Zarautz · Day 5 · 25 min from San Sebastián

Zarautz hosts surf competitions and beach parties across July and August — the promenade bars run late and the beach stays busy after dark. Your final morning surf session on Day 5 may land mid-competition, which makes for great spectating between waves.

🏄 ✦ Sun 20 Jul — your last morning
Outdoor Music July & August evenings

San Sebastián Summer Concert Series

La Concha Promenade & Zurriola · San Sebastián

Free outdoor concerts run along the waterfront throughout the summer — local bands, jazz combos, and occasional bigger acts on temporary stages. Check the Donostia Kultura listings when you arrive. A relaxed way to end an evening after the pintxos crawl.

🎵 ✦ Sat–Sun 18–19 Jul
Jazz ~22–27 Jul · starts 2 days after you leave

Jazzaldia — San Sebastián Jazz Festival

San Sebastián · your final base

One of the world's most prestigious jazz festivals, held on the waterfront stages of La Concha. Past headliners: Pat Metheny, Diana Krall, Herbie Hancock. You fly out on the 20th — staying two extra days would put you right in the opening weekend.

Monte Cobetas, Bilbao · 20 min from Casco Viejo

Bilbao's major rock and electronic festival on a hilltop overlooking the city. Past lineups have included Arctic Monkeys, Massive Attack, Rosalía. Arriving three days earlier would cover the full festival before the road trip begins.
